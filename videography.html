Learning to Notice Beauty

Throughout my experience creating videos, I've focused intently on story. At first it was learning to engage people on YouTube with solid storytelling, a curiosity inducing hook, and interesting edits. But as I learned more and matured creatively, I desired to tell more contemplative stories. And recently, I had the opportunity to focus on beauty and seek to integrate story without saying a single word. This challenge has inspired me to notice and capture beauty more in my work, and it has been a joy to integrate these two loves in what I do.

Capturing the Beauty of Christmas at STA Parish

It was beautiful and humbling to be able to record video in the presence of our Lord; trying to maintain a prayerful and respectful demeanor, while seeking to capture appealing shots.

For this video, my goal was to capture the beauty of the parish, so that someone who saw it could have an idea of what to expect from the Christmas season there. At the same time, I wanted to move people emotionally, reminding them of the hope we find in this season, and the One to whom we owe this hope.

To accomplish this, I relied on a gimbal to capture smooth shots while walking, and a tripod to get stable static shots. I recorded most of the footage in 4k at 60p, using a 10-bit color profile to achieve smooth footage and seamless transitions in the shadows and gradients of light. After filming, I color graded the S-Log3 footage in Davinci Resolve to achieve a warm, but accurate aesthetic.
